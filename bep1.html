KFC

KFC offers a variety of fried chicken, sandwiches, side dishes, desserts, and beverages. KFC’s most famous product is the Original Recipe, which is made with an eleven-spice secret recipe that they have guarded well.

KFC is a fast-food restraint chain. Its headquarters is in Louisville, Kentucky, and is one of several restaurant chains owned by !Yum Brands. “There are over 21,000 KFC outlets in more than 130 countries and territories in the world (KFC).”

KFC locations are placed in premium locations near malls and shopping complexes around the world. They now offer online ordering and delivery in some locations.
KFC starting pay for the average worker is $7.89 per hour. They offer 401k, tuition assistance, health insurance, and stock purchases. Most of KFC community efforts are concentrated outside of the United States. KFC Thailand, Germany, and United Kingdom all have programs geared towards exercise and eating healthy. KFC United Kingdom and Ireland are part of the Red Tractor farm assured scheme, which guarantees the chicken they use meet food, safety, and hygiene regulations, as well as, environmental and animal welfare standards. KFC United States has programs Reach, Rise, and Family Fund. Reach is their tuition reimbursement program for higher education, Rise is to aid employees without a high school diploma get their GED, and Family Fund is to aid employees and their families during hardship. KFC also participates in Add Hope, a program that fundraises and partners with the United Nations World Food Programme geared toward ending hunger.

Hot Chicken Takeover

Hot Chicken Takeover serves their own recipe of Nashville Style Hot Chicken, from mild to very spicy, and they offer a variety of side dishes, desserts, and beverages as well. Hot Chicken Takeover has three restaurant locations in Columbus, Ohio, owned by Joe Deloss. Each location is in popular shopping areas, Easton Gateway, North Market, and Clintonville.

Hot Chicken Takeover’s average starting pay is $11 per hour not including tips. They are a Fair Chance Employer, which means they give everyone consideration for employment. They are known to provide jobs for people needing a second chance, which is especially helpful to criminal records and homeless people. They have a 2:1 Matched Milestone Program, which helps with housing, education, transportation, etc. Other benefits are: food safety certification, counseling, cash advances, and health insurance. Hot Chicken Takeover involves itself in numerous community efforts such as Dress for Success Columbus, where they donate $5 for every shirt they sell. They encourage people to purchase the shirts by providing a free meal with purchase and $1 off whenever customers wear the shirt in the future. They recently paired up with Fusion to raise money for Pelotonia and raised over 40,000 meals for Food Rescue Us on National Fried Chicken Day. They also partnered with Run Down the Demons, a local non-profit whose goal is to educate the public about the difficulties and complications that veterans face. They made army green shirts for veterans day and donated $5 per shirt sold.
